Fujisawa's [insane sound direction]({{ site.url_prefixes.youtube.video }}/_4QFF0gZrVo) in [Land of the Lustrous]({{ site.url_prefixes.myanimelist.anime }}/35557) made me realise that music is much more than just complexity. And yes, [Cinnabar's Theme]({{ site.url_prefixes.youtube.video }}/4WzXIAzz1ZA) was instrumental in making Erhu one of my favourites.\
Fujisawa was mostly known for his classical-leaning style and found great success with the OSTs of [Mushoku Tensei]({{ site.url_prefixes.myanimelist.anime }}/39535), [A Place Further Than The Universe]({{ site.url_prefixes.myanimelist.anime }}/35839) and [Revue Starlight]({{ site.url_prefixes.myanimelist.anime }}/35503). But recently, he has switched things up with the [dyna]({{ site.url_prefixes.youtube.video }}/IDcufvofw8I)[mic]({{ site.url_prefixes.youtube.video }}/s70WJY4xnaw) [sco]({{ site.url_prefixes.youtube.video }}/pbbKaXkGXuk)[res]({{ site.url_prefixes.youtube.video }}/u-rUEZEEYSY) of _Takopi's Original Sin_ and [Apocalypse Hotel]({{ site.url_prefixes.myanimelist.anime }}/59675), perfectly matching their unique vibes.
